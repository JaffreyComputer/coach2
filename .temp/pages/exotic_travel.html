Belize - Central America

Once known as British Honduras, Belize in pre-Columbian times was part of the mighty Mayan empire. In the 21st century, uncommercialized environments are a rarity. Belize, situated on the Gulf of Mexico and to the east of Guatemala, offers both the jungle and the beach.

Belize's cayes are renowned for outstanding snorkeling and diving in pristine waters. Its jungles contain some of the richest examples of hardwood trees and medicinal plants as well as being home to thousands of varieties of butterflies, birds and amphibians.

This is an excellent alternative to Hawaii, Costa Rica or even Mexico for those seeking an exotic yet accessible vacation.
